# Lesson 03 - Bounding Box Quality & QA Review

## Objective

Learn how to evaluate the quality of bounding box annotations and identify common annotation errors.

---

## What I Learned

A high-quality bounding box should:

- Be tight around the object.
- Include only the correct object.
- Have the correct label.
- Follow the annotation guidelines.
- Avoid duplicate annotations.

---

## Common Annotation Errors

- Missing objects
- Wrong labels
- Duplicate bounding boxes
- Loose bounding boxes
- Annotating objects outside the project scope

---

## QA Practice Completed

### Challenge 1

Decision: Reject

Reason:
A tree was incorrectly labeled as a bus.

---

### Challenge 2

Decision: Reject

Reason:
Duplicate bounding boxes were created around the same car.

---

### Challenge 3

Decision: Reject

Reason:
The annotator labeled a tiny object even though the guidelines required annotating only large visible objects.

---

## Reflection

Today I learned how to review annotations like a QA reviewer. I now understand that quality annotation is not just about drawing boxes—it is also about following project guidelines, avoiding common errors, and providing clear reasons for QA decisions.# Lesson 03 - Bounding Box Quality & QA Review
